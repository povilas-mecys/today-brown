New Features in Today@Brown

In summer 2024, the Today@Brown team launched two important new features in direct response to input from the campus community — rich text formatting and full integration with Events@Brown.

Rich Text Formatting

When submitting announcements and events, you can now use rich text formatting, including:

  • Embedded links
  • Headings
  • Bulleted lists
  • Numbered lists
  • Bold text
  • Italicized text

Please continue to use simple text for the first sentence of your submission because Today@Brown automatically uses the first 100 characters of that sentence as the descriptor for your submission that appears in the email digest. In other words, avoid starting a message with a heading or a list.

Integration with Events@Brown

With a new integration with the Events@Brown calendar, the process for promoting an event in Today@Brown has been significantly streamlined. To date, promoting an event in Today@Brown has required entering the event twice — once in Events@Brown and then again in Today@Brown.

Now, when creating or editing events in Events@Brown, you will simply use the checkbox for “Submit this event to Today@Brown” and enter the event information.

Online Training for Event Submission
Here are a few tips related to the integration with Events@Brown:

You will not need to create a separate item about your event in Today@Brown, nor will you be able to as in the past — instead, your event entry in Events@Brown will flow into Today@Brown.

When creating or editing an event, you will have the option to designate second and third publication dates for your event to appear in Today@Brown.

If you don’t have a login for Events@Brown, you can use the general submission form and indicate with the checkbox that it should be promoted in Today@Brown.

If your event is rejected by the Today@Brown moderator, you can go back to Events@Brown to make the edits and/or changes required by the moderator.

Announcement Submissions Unchanged

For announcements — not events, which are distinct — you will still submit items to Today@Brown as you do currently. What’s the difference between an announcement and an event?

Per Today@Brown guidelines, an event has a specific location and a short-duration timeframe associated with it. For example, a performance to be held at 7:00 p.m. on Tuesday, July 9, at the Lindemann Performing Arts center is an event, because it takes place at a specific time.

An announcement is a notice about an initiative that may or may not be linked to a timeframe. For example, an all-day blood drive on the Campus Green is considered an announcement because it does not have a specific start and end time.
